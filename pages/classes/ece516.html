ECE 516
Wearable Computing and AR (Augmediated Reality) Eye Glass

Starts Jan. 2015, available to 3rd and 4th yr. undergrad or grad. students

Course Info

Course Text - "Intelligent Image Processing", by S. Mann, Wiley-Interscience

Course Website - http://wearcam.org/ece516/

Course Instructor - Prof. Steve Mann

Course Offering

  • Students get to build Wearable Computing devices for AR (Augmediated Reality)
  • You get to design the product ("Digital Eye Glass") that will replace most smartphones
  • Design technology integrating with the human body, form, and function
  • Wearable Computing is now a $241 billion industry
  • Want to invent the future? Become a modern-day Leonardo daVinci: Multidisciplinary thinker+world's best engineer
  • Make the world a better place, e.g. former students of course instructor S. Mann have gone on to start multimillion dollar multinational companies like InteraXon with this technology
  • We also have a small company that manufactures Digital Eye Glass: http://spaceglasses.com
  • Maktivism: Making physical objects and cloud-based computing apps. Goal: to make the world a better place.
  • Those enrolled for course credit will have opportunity to use and build various kinds of AR eyewear+smartphone apps.
    • The best students have the opportunity to present their work in Silicon Valley, California, and have some opportunities at Meta (spaceglasses.com). As Chief Scientist of Rotman School of Business CDL, Prof. Mann will be available to help top students with enterprise and entrepreneurship-related efforts. Learn how to do world-class research and publish papers in top-calibre IEEE conferences: See previous publictions here. Thanks to Martine Rothblatt for funding "The Steven Mann Award for Wearable Computing", a cash prize awared to the top undergraduate student in ECE516; last year's recipient, Nima Yasrebi, received the $2500 cash prize.
